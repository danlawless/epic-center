# Transformational Epicenter - Brand Guidelines

> *The visual expression of transformation*

---

## Brand Overview

### Mission
To create environments and experiences where people can remember who they really are - returning to life whole, present, sovereign, and awake.

### Vision
30+ Transformational Epicenters globally, united under one ethical, medical, and spiritual framework.

### Values
- **Safety** - Medical rigor and sacred containment
- **Transformation** - Deep, lasting change at the root
- **Luxury** - Care expressed through exceptional quality
- **Integration** - The full arc, not just the peak
- **Community** - Connection that sustains change

## Logo

### Primary Logo

The Transformational Epicenter logo represents:
- The nervous system (connection, coherence)
- Nature (organic, grounded)
- Transformation (movement, evolution)
- Unity (wholeness, integration)

*Logo files to be developed*

### Logo Variations

| Variation | Use Case |
|-----------|----------|
| Full color | Primary use, light backgrounds |
| Reversed | Dark backgrounds |
| Monochrome | Single color applications |
| Icon only | Favicon, app icon, small spaces |

### Clear Space

Minimum clear space around logo: height of logo "E"

### Minimum Size

- Print: 1 inch wide minimum
- Digital: 80px wide minimum

### Logo Don'ts

- Don't rotate or skew
- Don't change colors
- Don't add effects
- Don't crowd with other elements
- Don't place on busy backgrounds

## Color

### Primary Palette

**Deep Forest** - Primary Brand Color
- Hex: #1A3A3A
- RGB: 26, 58, 58
- Use: Primary buttons, headers, key elements

**Warm Gold** - Accent Color
- Hex: #C4A962
- RGB: 196, 169, 98
- Use: Highlights, CTAs, important elements

**Warm White** - Background
- Hex: #FAFAF8
- RGB: 250, 250, 248
- Use: Page backgrounds, breathing room

### Usage Ratios

- Primary (Deep Forest): 60%
- Neutral (Warm tones): 30%
- Accent (Warm Gold): 10%

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice

**Authoritative but Warm**
- We know what we're doing
- We care deeply about you
- Medical precision, human heart

**Clear but Not Clinical**
- Accessible language
- No jargon barriers
- Technical when needed

**Aspirational but Grounded**
- Transformation is possible
- It requires real work
- We're here with you

**Reverent but Not Precious**
- This is sacred work
- We're not performative
- Authenticity over aesthetics

### Tone Guidelines

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Medical content | Precise, clear, reassuring |
| Marketing | Aspirational, warm, inviting |
| Onboarding | Supportive, encouraging, clear |
| Integration | Gentle, patient, affirming |
| Community | Connected, inclusive, respectful |

### Writing Principles

1. **Be direct** - Say what you mean
2. **Be human** - Write like you speak
3. **Be respectful** - Honor the reader
4. **Be clear** - Prioritize understanding
5. **Be honest** - Don't oversell

## Imagery

### Photography Style

**Subject Matter**
- Nature and landscapes
- Architecture and interiors
- Authentic human moments
- Texture and materials
- Light and shadow

**Treatment**
- Natural light preferred
- Warm, soft color grading
- Not oversaturated
- Not over-processed
- Genuine, not staged

**What to Avoid**
- Stock photo clichés
- Forced smiles
- Cold, clinical settings
- Over-dramatic lighting
- Busy, cluttered scenes

### Image Examples

| Use | Style |
|-----|-------|
| Hero images | Sweeping nature, soft light |
| About pages | Authentic team moments |
| Program pages | Serene spaces, details |
| Testimonials | Natural portraits |
| Blog | Varied, editorial |
