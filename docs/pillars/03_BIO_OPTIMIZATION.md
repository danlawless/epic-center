## Core Modalities

### Thermal Therapies

#### Infrared Sauna

**What It Is**
Infrared saunas use light waves to heat the body directly, penetrating deeper than traditional saunas while operating at lower ambient temperatures.

**Benefits**
- Deep tissue heating
- Increased circulation
- Detoxification support
- Cardiovascular conditioning
- Relaxation response activation
- Pain reduction

**Protocol**
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Temperature | 45-60°C (113-140°F) |
| Duration | 20-40 minutes |
| Frequency | Daily available |
| Hydration | Pre and post required |

**Contraindications**
- Recent alcohol/substance use
- Acute illness with fever
- Severe cardiovascular disease
- Pregnancy
- Open wounds

#### Traditional Sauna

**What It Is**
Finnish-style sauna using heated stones and steam for ambient heating.

**Benefits**
- Cardiovascular exercise mimetic
- Immune system activation
- Social/community experience
- Heat shock protein activation
- Mental resilience building

**Protocol**
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Temperature | 70-90°C (158-194°F) |
| Duration | 10-20 minutes |
| Rounds | 2-4 with cooling between |
| Frequency | Available daily |

### Cold Therapy

#### Cold Plunge

**What It Is**
Immersion in cold water (typically 1-10°C / 34-50°F) for brief periods.

**Benefits**
- Vagal tone activation
- Dopamine and norepinephrine increase
- Inflammation reduction
- Mental resilience building
- Nervous system reset
- Mood enhancement

**Protocol**
| Level | Temperature | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Beginner | 10-15°C | 30-60 seconds |
| Intermediate | 5-10°C | 1-3 minutes |
| Advanced | 1-5°C | 2-5 minutes |

**Breathing Protocol**
- Calm breath before entry
- Controlled exhalation upon entry
- Slow nasal breathing during
- Recovery breathing after

**Contraindications**
- Uncontrolled hypertension
- Severe cardiovascular disease
- Raynaud's disease (severe)
- Cold urticaria
- Recent heart attack

#### Contrast Therapy

**What It Is**
Alternating between heat (sauna) and cold (plunge) for enhanced benefits.

**Protocol**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                     CONTRAST THERAPY PROTOCOL                           │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                          │
│  1. Warm up gently (5 min)                                              │
│  2. Sauna: 10-15 minutes                                                │
│  3. Cold plunge: 1-3 minutes                                            │
│  4. Rest: 5-10 minutes                                                  │
│  5. Repeat 2-4 rounds                                                   │
│  6. End with desired temperature                                        │
│  7. Rest and hydrate                                                    │
│                                                                          │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Light Therapies

#### Red Light Therapy (Photobiomodulation)

**What It Is**
Exposure to red (630-660nm) and near-infrared (810-850nm) light for cellular benefits.

**Benefits**
- Mitochondrial function enhancement
- Collagen production
- Wound healing
- Inflammation reduction
- Skin health
- Potential cognitive benefits

**Protocol**
| Body Area | Distance | Duration |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Full body | 6-18 inches | 10-20 minutes |
| Targeted | 4-8 inches | 5-10 minutes |
| Face | 6-12 inches | 10-15 minutes |

**Frequency**: Daily available, recommended 3-5x/week

**Best Timing**
- Morning: Energizing
- Evening: Recovery focus
- Post-exercise: Recovery

### Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)

**What It Is**
Breathing 100% oxygen in a pressurized chamber, increasing oxygen delivery to tissues.

**Benefits**
- Enhanced tissue oxygenation
- Reduced inflammation
- Wound healing
- Neuroplasticity support
- Cognitive enhancement
- Stem cell mobilization

**Protocol**
| Parameter | Mild | Standard |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Pressure | 1.3-1.5 ATA | 2.0-2.4 ATA |
| Duration | 60-90 minutes | 60-90 minutes |
| Frequency | 5x/week | Per protocol |

**During Session**
- Rest or meditation
- No electronics (fire risk)
- Equalize ear pressure
- Comfortable clothing

**Contraindications**
- Untreated pneumothorax
- Certain lung conditions
- Ear/sinus problems
- Claustrophobia (relative)
- Recent ear surgery

### IV Therapy & Ozone

#### IV Nutrient Therapy

**What It Is**
Intravenous delivery of vitamins, minerals, and nutrients for enhanced absorption.

**Common Protocols**
| Protocol | Contents | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Myers Cocktail | B vitamins, C, Mg, Ca | General wellness |
| Immune Boost | High-dose C, Zn, Glutathione | Immune support |
| NAD+ | NAD+ precursors | Cellular energy, longevity |
| Glutathione | Glutathione | Detox, antioxidant |
| Hydration | Electrolytes, fluids | Recovery, rehydration |

**Timing Considerations**
- Pre-ceremony: Hydration, nutrient optimization
- Post-ceremony: Recovery, replenishment
- General: Per individual assessment

#### Ozone Therapy

**What It Is**
Medical-grade ozone (O3) delivered through various methods for therapeutic benefit.

**Methods**
- Major Autohemotherapy (MAH): Blood drawn, ozonated, reinfused
- Minor Autohemotherapy: Smaller blood volume, IM injection
- Rectal insufflation: Non-invasive option

**Benefits**
- Immune modulation
- Oxygen utilization enhancement
- Antimicrobial properties
- Circulation improvement

**Note**: Requires physician oversight and proper protocols.
